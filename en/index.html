Olmo Gómez Aldaz Case

This site brings together materials, documents and reflections around a process of identity restitution initiated in adulthood and recognised by the courts.

Not as a closed narrative, but as an archive under construction.

The story

A biography shaped by false records, prolonged absences and a historical context that explains how certain practices were possible. This section situates the facts, the silences and the documents needed to understand the origin of the case and the need to tell it.

View the story

The sentence

The judicial ruling is not presented as a final point, but as a milestone within a broader process. The recognition of biological filiation without annulling the adoptive one introduces an unprecedented legal situation in Spain and opens a new framework from which to observe its legal and personal implications.

The books

The books linked to this project do not follow a single register or a closed intention. They arise from the need to organise materials, lived experience, and reflections that do not fit within a single format. Writing, memory, and archive intersect as part of the same process.

View the books

The author

The figure of the author is inseparable from the process of identity restitution itself. Rather than a linear trajectory, what emerges is a path shaped by the re-examination of the past, access to documentation, and the reconstruction of one’s own name from the present.

Press

The media coverage is part of the development of the case. The different reports make it possible to observe how the facts are translated into a public narrative and how each outlet introduces its own nuances, emphases, and framing. Bringing them together here allows that evolution to be followed.
