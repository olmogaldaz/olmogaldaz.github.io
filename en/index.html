Olmo Gómez Aldaz Case

This site brings together materials, documents and reflections around a process of identity restitution initiated in adulthood and recognised by the courts.

Not as a closed narrative, but as an archive under construction.

The story

A biography shaped by false records, prolonged absences and a historical context that explains how certain practices were possible. This section situates the facts, the silences and the documents needed to understand the origin of the case and the need to tell it.

View the story

The legal claim

The legal claim sets out the attempt to restore identity through judicial means. It brings together the facts, the documentation submitted, and the legal grounds supporting the request for the restitution of name, filiation, and registral truth.

Access the claim

The sentence

The judicial ruling is not presented as a final point, but as a milestone within a broader process. The recognition of biological filiation without annulling the adoptive one introduces an unprecedented legal situation in Spain and opens a new framework from which to observe its legal and personal implications.

The books

The books connected to this project do not belong to a single register or a closed intention. They arise from the need to organise materials, lived experience, and reflections that do not fit into a single format. Writing, memory, and archive intersect as part of the same process.

The author

The author cannot be separated from the process of identity restitution itself. Rather than a linear trajectory, what emerges is a path shaped by revisiting the past, gaining access to documentation, and rebuilding one’s own name from the present.
